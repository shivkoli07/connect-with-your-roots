Territorial Evolution of the Maratha Empire

Maratha Territory at the Time of Shivaji Maharaj (1680)

At the time of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj’s death in 1680, the Marathas controlled Konkan, Satara, Kolhapur, and parts of Karnataka and Tamil regions.

Maratha Revival after Sambhaji Maharaj (1700)

Despite Mughal oppression, the Marathas regrouped and revived their strength after 1689.

Maratha Expansion Across India (1785)

The Marathas emerged as the paramount power, extending influence across most of India.

Southern Expansion and Power (1798)

Victory over the Nizam at Kharda extended Maratha dominance in the Deccan.

Anglo-Maratha Wars (1802–1805)

The Treaty of Bassein weakened Maratha sovereignty and strengthened British control.

Collapse of Maratha Independence (1819–1836)

The British dismantled Maratha autonomy after the Third Anglo-Maratha War.

Doctrine of Lapse & Final Annexations (1856)

Former Maratha states were annexed under British imperial policy.
